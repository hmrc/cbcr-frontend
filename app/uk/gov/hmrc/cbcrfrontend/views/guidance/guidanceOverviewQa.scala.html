Guidance

Check if you must send a country-by-country report

Published:
17 May 2017
Last updated:
20 June 2017, see all updates

Qualifying multinational groups must send HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) a report each year on certain aspects of their business activities.

Notify HMRC that you’re required to send a country-by-country report.

Register to use the country-by-country reporting service.

Send a report using the reporting service.

The UK follows the country-by-country reporting requirements of the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD). You’ll have to send HMRC a report if you meet certain criteria.

Groups which must complete a report

The reporting requirements apply to multinational enterprises with a consolidated revenue of €750 million or more in the relevant accounting period.

You can find information on the qualifying criteria, and other areas, in the International Exchange of Information Manual.

Group’s reporting member

For HMRC’s purposes, this will be if you’re any of the following:

  • based in the UK, and you’re the parent business of the group in question
  • the highest level UK business of an enterprise based in a country that either:
    • doesn’t require your multinational enterprise to file a country-by-country report
    • has entered into a tax information exchange agreement, but with no country-by-country reporting requirements
    • has information exchange agreements already in place between it and the UK, but HMRC has notified you that they aren’t working effectively

You can also volunteer to send HMRC a report if you aren’t required to do so.

Notifications

If your group needs to send a report, you must tell HMRC each year at the end of your accounting period:

  • that this is the case
  • who will be sending the report
  • which country the report will be sent to

You do this by emailing notification.cbcrfiling hmrc.gsi.gov.uk.

Don’t send your country-by-country report to the notification mailbox.

You will have until 1 September 2017 to do so if this is later than your relevant accounting period.

If you have an HMRC Customer Relationship Manager (CRM), copy them into your email.

If you’ve told HMRC you’ll need to send a country-by-country report to us, you’ll need to register if you haven’t already done so.

Send your report

You’ll have to upload a valid, completed XML schema as part of the country-by-country reporting process.

You must follow these rules when completing the schema.

In order to send HMRC your report, you’ll use the reporting service.

HMRC recommends you register at least 1 day before you need to send the report as you’ll need the CbCID number you’ll receive from registering in order to complete your XML schema.

Deadline

You must have sent your report within 12 months from the end of your relevant accounting period.

If you need to correct or amend your report after you send it to HMRC, you have 3 years from the end of the relevant accounting period to do so.

Penalties

You could be charged a penalty for late filing or inaccuracies in your country-by-country report. You may be charged:

  • £300 for failing to notify HMRC by the notification deadline, with a daily penalty of up to £60
  • £300 for failing to report by the reporting deadline, with a daily penalty of up to £60
  • up to £3,000 for an inaccurate report

If there’s an error in your report, you can send a replacement schema by using the reporting service again.

You won’t receive a penalty for an inaccurate report where you’ve sent an accurate replacement schema before the deadline.

Contact us

If you already have an HMRC CRM, they should be your first point of contact for any questions in relation to:

  • notifications
  • the registration service
  • the reporting service
  • HMRC’s rules for completing the XML schema

If you don’t have a CRM, you can email [mailbox].

Further information

You can find more details on the above in the International Exchange of Information Manual.
