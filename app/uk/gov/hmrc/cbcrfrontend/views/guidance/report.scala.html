Send a country-by-country report using the reporting service.

Before you can send your report to HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC), you’ll need to:

  • register your business with the service
  • make sure your report follows HMRC’s XML (extensible markup language) schema rules

Before you start

To send your country-by-country report you’ll need your multinational group’s:

  • country-by-country ID (CbCID) - this is the number that was generated when you completed your business’s registration
  • HMRC unique taxpayer reference (UTR)
  • registered name and postcode matching the UTR
  • contact details for the person responsible for sending the report

If you don’t control your multinational group, you’ll need the name of your group parent business.

You’ll need a completed country-by-country report that uses the XML schema. You may need to ask a colleague with knowledge of XML to create the file for you to send.

You must make sure:

  • the filename matches MessageRefID
  • the file’s size is no bigger than 50MB
  • it contains your CbCID number in the SendingEntityIN
  • it must end in .xml

Information to report

Information on your report will broadly include:

  • revenue
  • profit before income tax
  • income tax paid and accrued
  • total number of employees
  • capital
  • retained earnings
  • assets

You must also identify each business within your multinational group, trading in each tax jurisdiction, along with their broad business activities.

Send your report

You must send your business’s report to HMRC using the country-by-country reporting service.

Change your report

If there’s a mistake in your report, you can send a newly generated, full XML file to replace it. HMRC will use the most recent report you’ve sent.

You have 3 years from the end of the relevant accounting period to replace a report.

Agents

As an agent of a business, you can send their report for them.

To do this, you’ll need to confirm their details by using their HMRC UTR, and their postcode related to that UTR. You can then move onto the normal reporting process.

Further information

You can find more details on the above in the International Exchange of Information Manual.
